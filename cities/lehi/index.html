12 Things You Need to Know About Lehi

A curated data profile for families and professionals looking to establish roots here.

Transit & Proximity

  • 1. I-15 Corridor AccessLehi offers direct and convenient access to Interstate 15, making commutes throughout the Wasatch Front highly efficient for residents. This prime location is key to its growth and connectivity.
  • 2. Salt Lake City ProximityDowntown Salt Lake City is typically a 30-35 minute drive from Lehi, placing a major urban center within easy reach for work, culture, and entertainment.
  • 3. Provo City AccessLehi is located approximately 20-25 minutes north of Provo City, providing convenient access to Brigham Young University and other amenities in southern Utah County.
  • 4. Silicon Slopes CoreLehi is the undeniable heart of Utah's 'Silicon Slopes,' home to numerous tech giants and startups, defining its economic and cultural landscape.
  • 5. FrontRunner StationThe city is served by a UTA FrontRunner commuter rail station, offering an alternative transportation option for travel between Ogden and Provo.
  • 6. Airport Commute TimeSalt Lake City International Airport (SLC) is approximately a 35-40 minute drive from Lehi, ensuring convenient air travel for residents.

Demographics & Community

  • 7. One of Fastest GrowingLehi has consistently ranked as one of the fastest-growing cities in Utah, experiencing exponential population growth over the past two decades.
  • 8. Young Median AgeThe city boasts a significantly young median age, reflecting its appeal to families and young professionals drawn by job opportunities and new housing.
  • 9. Alpine School DistrictLehi is part of the Alpine School District, one of the largest and most highly-regarded public school districts in Utah, serving a diverse student body.
  • 10. Thanksgiving Point HubThanksgiving Point, a 500-acre complex of gardens, museums, and attractions, is a major cultural and recreational landmark within Lehi.
  • 11. Historic Downtown AreaWhile known for new development, Lehi also features a charming historic downtown area that preserves some of the city's pioneer heritage.
  • 12. Master-Planned CommunitiesMuch of Lehi's residential landscape comprises modern, master-planned communities designed with amenities, parks, and family-friendly living in mind.

What We Love About Lehi (And You Will Too)

Lehi residents cherish the vibrant, forward-thinking atmosphere fueled by the 'Silicon Slopes' tech boom. It's a city where innovative careers meet a family-friendly lifestyle, offering abundant new construction homes and master-planned communities. The access to outdoor recreation, from Utah Lake to nearby canyons, complements the sophisticated urban amenities like world-class museums and diverse dining. The strong sense of community, combined with excellent schools and breathtaking mountain views, makes Lehi a highly desirable place to live for those seeking growth, opportunity, and a high quality of life in Utah County.

Lehi: Where Innovation Meets Family Life

Lehi distinguishes itself from other Utah County cities through its rapid growth and relentless focus on technological innovation, earning its moniker 'Silicon Slopes.' Unlike some of the more established, historic cities to its south, Lehi offers a landscape dominated by modern architecture, master-planned communities, and state-of-the-art corporate campuses. This unique blend attracts a dynamic, often younger demographic seeking career opportunities coupled with exceptional family amenities and a forward-looking community vision. It's less about quaint Main Street charm and more about dynamic expansion and contemporary living.

A Day in Lehi: The Local Routine

AM

Morning: Active Start

A typical Lehi morning often starts with the glow of the sun hitting the Wasatch Front as residents prepare for their day. Commuters head to the I-15 corridor, many destined for the thriving tech companies within Silicon Slopes, while others utilize the FrontRunner train. School drop-offs are a bustling affair at the various Alpine School District campuses. Before work or after the kids are settled, a quick stop at a local coffee shop like Black Rifle Coffee Company or a home brew provides the perfect start, often accompanied by crisp mountain air and stunning views.

12P

Afternoon: Local Hubs

Afternoons in Lehi often revolve around outdoor activities and errands. Children flock to one of the many community parks, like the popular Lehi Sports Park or Dry Creek Lake Park, for sports practice or playground fun. Parents might tackle grocery shopping at Harmons or Smiths, or browse the retail offerings at Traverse Mountain Outlets. For a unique afternoon outing, the Museum of Natural Curiosity at Thanksgiving Point offers engaging experiences, providing an enriching blend of education and entertainment before evening routines begin.

PM

Evening: Family & Community

As evening approaches, Lehi takes on a family-centric rhythm. Many families gather for dinner at home, enjoying meals prepared in their modern kitchens. For those looking to dine out, options range from casual eateries like Strap Tank Brewery to diverse international cuisine. Community events, especially during warmer months, often bring residents together at local parks or the Lehi Legacy Center. A quiet walk or bike ride along the Jordan River Parkway offers a serene end to the day, with city lights twinkling against the mountain backdrop.

Schools, Dining & Local Infrastructure

The Education Standard

Lehi is part of the highly-regarded Alpine School District, one of Utah's largest and most comprehensive districts. It boasts several top-tier schools, including Skyridge High School and Lehi High School, along with numerous middle and elementary schools like Willowcreek Middle and Traverse Mountain Elementary, known for strong academic programs and extracurricular offerings. The city's infrastructure is built to support its rapid growth, featuring excellent road networks with direct access to I-15 for efficient regional travel. Public transportation via the UTA FrontRunner commuter rail further enhances connectivity, making it easy for residents to navigate beyond city limits for work or leisure.

Local Dining & Culture

Lehi's dining scene reflects its modern, diverse population, offering a blend of fast-casual, unique local spots, and elevated experiences. For a lively atmosphere and craft brews, Strap Tank Brewery is a local favorite, serving hearty American fare. Those seeking healthy, fresh options frequent Aubergine & Company, known for its Mediterranean-inspired menu. Sushi enthusiasts rave about Roll With It, a popular spot for creative rolls. Beyond these, you'll find everything from classic burger joints and pizza parlors to emerging ethnic restaurants, particularly around the Traverse Mountain area, catering to every palate and occasion.

Favorite Weekend Spots

Weekends in Lehi are packed with opportunities for recreation and relaxation, often centered around Thanksgiving Point. Here, families can explore the Museum of Natural Curiosity, stroll through the vibrant Ashton Gardens, or step back in time at Farm Country. For retail therapy and entertainment, the Traverse Mountain Outlets provide a wide array of shops and dining options. Outdoor enthusiasts frequently head to the nearby Jordan River Parkway for biking, jogging, or enjoying nature. During warmer months, Utah Lake offers boating and fishing opportunities just a short drive away. For a change of scenery, the majestic American Fork Canyon is easily accessible, providing hiking trails and scenic drives.
