12 Things to Know
About Lehi

A curated profile for families and professionals looking to put down roots here.

Transit & Proximity

  • 1. I-15 AccessLehi is strategically located directly on I-15, Utah's main north-south freeway, offering excellent connectivity to Salt Lake City and Provo.
  • 2. SLC CommuteThe average commute time from Lehi to downtown Salt Lake City is approximately 35-45 minutes, depending on traffic conditions.
  • 3. Provo ProximityLehi is just about a 15-20 minute drive north of Provo, making it convenient for those commuting to Brigham Young University or Utah Valley University.
  • 4. FrontRunner StationLehi hosts a UTA FrontRunner commuter rail station, providing an alternative transit option for travel along the Wasatch Front.
  • 5. Airport AccessSalt Lake City International Airport (SLC) is approximately 35 miles north of Lehi, typically a 30-40 minute drive.
  • 6. Traverse Mountain TunnelThe Timpanogos Highway (SR-92) features a tunnel through Traverse Mountain, significantly improving access to Draper and Highland.

Demographics & Community

  • 7. Silicon Slopes HubLehi is widely recognized as the heart of "Silicon Slopes," Utah's burgeoning tech industry hub, home to companies like Adobe and Micron.
  • 8. Rapid GrowthLehi has been one of the fastest-growing cities in Utah and the nation over the past two decades, with its population surging past 85,000 residents.
  • 9. Thanksgiving PointThis 500-acre non-profit provides year-round family entertainment, including museums, gardens, and a golf course, drawing visitors from across the state.
  • 10. Historic Main StreetDespite rapid growth, Lehi maintains a charming historic Main Street area with older buildings and local businesses, contrasting its modern tech centers.
  • 11. Family-Friendly CityWith a median age around 25-30, Lehi is a remarkably young and family-oriented community, evidenced by its numerous parks and strong school system.
  • 12. Alpine School DistrictLehi falls within the Alpine School District, one of the largest school districts in Utah, known for its strong academic and athletic programs.

our take

Lehi: Where Innovation Fuels Family Life

Lehi stands apart in Utah County as the epicenter of the tech boom, giving it a distinctly modern and energetic vibe compared to its more established, often agricultural neighbors. While retaining Utah's core family values, Lehi boasts a younger, more transient population driven by tech careers, fostering a dynamic culture of innovation and growth. It's less about quaint downtowns and more about master-planned communities, cutting-edge corporate campuses, and a forward-thinking approach to urban development, offering a unique blend of high-tech opportunity and accessible outdoor recreation.

a typical day

Sunrise to Sundown in Lehi

Morning

Active Start

Mornings in Lehi buzz with purposeful energy. Families navigate I-15 for commutes to tech campuses or drive along busy main roads for school drop-offs at places like Skyridge High. Many grab a quick coffee at local favorites like Black Rifle Coffee or Beans & Brews before diving into the day. The crisp mountain air often carries the promise of a productive day, with the majestic Wasatch Front providing a stunning backdrop to the awakening city.

Afternoon

Local Hubs

As the workday winds down, Lehi's parks, like Lehi Sports Park or Olympic Park, fill with kids' sports teams and families enjoying the open space. Shoppers flock to the Outlets at Traverse Mountain for retail therapy, while others run errands at the various shopping centers that dot the landscape. After-school activities are plentiful, from dance studios to tutoring centers, reflecting the city's commitment to youth development and recreation.

Evening

Family Time

Evenings in Lehi are typically family-focused. Dinner at home, perhaps with takeout from a local eatery, is common. Many families take advantage of community events at Thanksgiving Point or movie nights at the megaplex. Neighborhoods come alive with evening strolls, and friendly faces can be seen gathering on porches. The city offers a balanced pace, ensuring residents can unwind and connect after a busy day.

the local pulse

Schools, Dining & Weekend Escapes

The Education Standard

Lehi is proudly served by the Alpine School District, one of Utah's largest and highest-performing districts. The city boasts a robust educational infrastructure, including modern high schools like Skyridge High School and Lehi High School, along with numerous middle and elementary schools such as Willowcreek Middle and Eaglecrest Elementary, known for their strong academic programs. Transportation infrastructure is dominated by I-15, which bisects the city, providing critical connectivity for commuters. Local roads are constantly being upgraded to manage the rapid population growth, ensuring residents have efficient access to schools, amenities, and employment centers.

Local Dining & Culture

Lehi's dining scene is a delicious reflection of its growth, offering a mix of casual eateries and emerging upscale options. For a unique experience, Strap Tank Brewery provides excellent craft beer and pub fare in a lively setting. Hearth on 25th offers a more refined, farm-to-table experience with a cozy atmosphere. Casual favorites include local pizza joints, various international cuisines like Roll with It for Japanese fusion, and quick bites found in the bustling shopping centers near Traverse Mountain. From family-friendly diners to trendy new spots, Lehi's culinary landscape is continually expanding.

Favorite Weekend Spots

Weekends in Lehi are packed with activities for all ages. Thanksgiving Point is a major draw, offering world-class attractions like the Museum of Natural Curiosity, Farm Country, Ashton Gardens, and the Butterfly Biosphere—perfect for a full day of exploration. For shopping enthusiasts, the Outlets at Traverse Mountain provide a wide array of retail options. Outdoor adventurers can hit the numerous hiking and biking trails accessible from Lehi, including those leading into Dry Creek Canyon, or enjoy water sports on nearby Utah Lake. Families also love Evermore Park, especially during seasonal events, for an immersive fantasy experience.
