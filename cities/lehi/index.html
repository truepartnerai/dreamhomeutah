the field guide

12 Things to Know
About Lehi

A curated profile for families and professionals looking to put down roots here.

Transit & Proximity

  • 1. I-15 Corridor AccessLehi sits directly on the I-15 corridor, providing efficient north-south travel throughout the Wasatch Front. This highway is vital for daily commutes and regional access.
  • 2. Salt Lake City CommuteA typical commute from Lehi to downtown Salt Lake City is approximately 30-40 minutes, depending on traffic conditions. This makes it a feasible option for SLC-based employment.
  • 3. FrontRunner StationLehi hosts a FrontRunner commuter rail station, offering an alternative transportation option to both Salt Lake City and Provo. It's a convenient choice for those preferring public transit.
  • 4. Utah Lake ProximityThe western edge of Lehi borders Utah Lake, offering residents easy access to boating, fishing, and various water recreation activities. Several marinas are just a short drive away.
  • 5. Traverse Mountain LocationLehi is nestled against the Traverse Mountain range, providing stunning views and immediate access to hiking and biking trails. It serves as a natural barrier and a recreational playground.
  • 6. Provo City AccessLehi is strategically located about 20 minutes north of Provo, making it an easy drive to BYU, UVU, and other South Utah County amenities. This central location is a major draw.

Demographics & Community

  • 7. Silicon Slopes HeartLehi is widely recognized as the epicenter of Utah's "Silicon Slopes" tech industry. Many major tech companies have their headquarters or significant offices here, driving economic growth.
  • 8. Rapid Population GrowthLehi has been one of the fastest-growing cities in Utah over the past two decades. Its population has surged due to job opportunities and attractive living conditions, reflecting a dynamic community.
  • 9. Alpine School DistrictStudents in Lehi attend schools within the Alpine School District, the largest K-12 public school district in Utah. The district is known for its high standards and comprehensive programs.
  • 10. Thanksgiving Point HubLehi is home to Thanksgiving Point, a major cultural and recreational complex featuring museums, gardens, and dining. It serves as a significant community asset and tourist attraction.
  • 11. Young DemographicThe city boasts a notably young and family-oriented demographic, with a median age typically lower than the state average. This contributes to a vibrant, active community.
  • 12. Historic Main StreetDespite its rapid modernization, Lehi preserves a charming historic Main Street area. This district offers a glimpse into the city's pioneer past amidst its futuristic growth.

our take

Lehi: Where Innovation Meets Inspired Living

Lehi stands apart from many traditional Utah County cities due to its intense focus on innovation and future-forward development. While other cities may boast quaint, established neighborhoods, Lehi is characterized by its dynamic growth, modern infrastructure, and a palpable energy stemming from the tech industry. It’s less about old-world charm and more about cutting-edge living, with a younger, often highly educated demographic drawn by career opportunities. This has led to a rich array of newer housing options, state-of-the-art amenities, and a fast-paced, optimistic outlook that truly sets it apart as the pulse of the Silicon Slopes.

a typical day

Sunrise to Sundown in Lehi

Morning

Active Start

Mornings in Lehi buzz with activity as families prepare for the day. Commuters often navigate I-15 heading north or south to tech campuses, while school buses and parent drop-offs fill the streets around Alpine School District's many institutions. Coffee aficionados grab a quick brew at local spots like Junction Coffee or Starbucks, often with stunning views of Traverse Mountain awakening under the morning sun. The air is crisp, and the atmosphere is generally optimistic, reflecting the city's forward-moving energy.

Afternoon

Local Hubs

Afternoons in Lehi often revolve around outdoor recreation and retail therapy. Children flock to one of the city's many well-maintained parks, such as Lehi Legacy Park or Ivory Ridge Park, for playtime and organized sports practices. Shoppers explore the diverse offerings at The Outlets at Traverse Mountain, while others might visit Thanksgiving Point for a stroll through the Ashton Gardens or a visit to the Museum of Natural Curiosity. The city's strategic location ensures that daily errands and leisure activities are always within easy reach.

Evening

Family Time

As evening descends on Lehi, families gather, and the city takes on a more relaxed, communal vibe. Many enjoy dinner at home or explore the growing number of dining options around Thanksgiving Point, from casual eateries to more upscale experiences. Community centers often host events and classes, while local sports fields light up with evening games. For some, a quiet walk along a neighborhood trail, enjoying the mountain views and vibrant sunsets, provides the perfect end to a busy Lehi day.

the local pulse

Schools, Dining & Weekend Escapes

The Education Standard

Lehi is served by the highly regarded Alpine School District, one of Utah's largest and most progressive districts, known for its strong academic programs and modern facilities. Key institutions include Lehi High School and Skyridge High School, both offering robust curricula and extensive extracurricular activities. The city's infrastructure is continually expanding to support its rapid growth, with significant investments in road improvements, including crucial I-15 access points, ensuring smooth transportation. New schools are frequently constructed to accommodate the influx of families, reflecting the city's commitment to providing excellent educational resources and efficient services for its residents.

Local Dining & Culture

Lehi's dining scene reflects its modern and diverse population, offering a blend of familiar favorites and unique local gems. Thanksgiving Point serves as a culinary hub, featuring restaurants like Harvest Restaurant, known for its farm-to-table approach, and Slab Pizza, a local favorite. For a more upscale experience, nearby Settebello Pizzeria Napoletana offers authentic Neapolitan pizza, while family-friendly options abound throughout the city, from traditional American fare to ethnic cuisine. Fast-casual dining is prevalent, catering to busy professionals and families, ensuring there's always something delicious to satisfy every palate.

Favorite Weekend Spots

Weekends in Lehi are an invitation to explore the city's premier attractions and stunning natural surroundings. Thanksgiving Point is a perennial favorite, offering a vast array of activities including the Museum of Natural Curiosity, Farm Country, and the breathtaking Ashton Gardens, perfect for family outings or a romantic stroll. Shoppers flock to The Outlets at Traverse Mountain for retail therapy, while outdoor enthusiasts can hit the scenic Jordan River Parkway Trail for biking or walking, or venture into the nearby Traverse Mountains for hiking adventures. With numerous community parks, playgrounds, and seasonal events, Lehi ensures there's always a new experience waiting around every corner.
