the field guide

12 Things to Know
About Lehi

A curated profile for families and professionals looking to put down roots here.

Transit & Proximity

  • 1. I-15 Corridor AccessLehi is strategically located along Interstate 15, providing direct and efficient access north to Salt Lake City and south to Provo. This prime location is a key factor in its growth and connectivity.
  • 2. SLC Commute TimeThe average commute time from Lehi to downtown Salt Lake City is approximately 30-40 minutes, depending on traffic conditions. This makes Lehi a viable option for those working in the state capital.
  • 3. UVX Bus Rapid TransitThe Utah Valley Express (UVX) bus rapid transit system extends into Lehi, offering convenient public transportation options. It connects key areas, including Utah Valley University and Brigham Young University.
  • 4. Thanksgiving Point HubThanksgiving Point, a major cultural and entertainment complex, is located entirely within Lehi. It houses museums, gardens, and farms, drawing visitors from across the state.
  • 5. Traverse Mountain ShoppingLehi's Traverse Mountain area features a prominent shopping outlet and numerous retail centers. This provides residents with extensive local shopping and dining options without needing to travel far.
  • 6. Provo ProximityLehi is just a 15-20 minute drive north of Provo, offering quick access to BYU and additional cultural and recreational amenities in Utah County's largest city.

Demographics & Community

  • 7. Silicon Slopes AnchorLehi is widely recognized as the epicenter of 'Silicon Slopes,' Utah's booming tech industry. Many major tech companies, including Adobe and Ancestry, have significant campuses here.
  • 8. Rapid Population GrowthLehi has been one of Utah's fastest-growing cities over the past two decades, with its population surging from under 20,000 in 2000 to well over 80,000 today. This growth continues steadily.
  • 9. Alpine School DistrictThe city falls within the highly-regarded Alpine School District, one of the largest in Utah. It is known for its strong academic programs and modern educational facilities.
  • 10. Pioneer History RootsFounded in 1850, Lehi is one of the oldest cities in Utah County, originally settled by pioneers. Its rich history is celebrated through various landmarks and community events.
  • 11. Average Household SizeLehi boasts a higher-than-average household size, reflecting its family-friendly environment. The typical household consists of around 3.5 people, indicative of its vibrant family communities.
  • 12. City of Lehi Legacy CenterThe Lehi Legacy Center is a comprehensive recreation facility offering pools, fitness equipment, and sports courts. It serves as a central hub for community health and wellness activities.

our take

Where Innovation Thrives and Community Connects

Lehi distinguishes itself from other Utah County cities through its rapid, intentional growth centered around the tech industry. Unlike the more established, sometimes slower-paced communities to the south, Lehi is a hub of innovation, attracting a diverse, often younger, and highly educated demographic. It's less about historic downtown charm and more about modern amenities, master-planned communities, and state-of-the-art facilities. This forward-looking energy creates a unique living experience, blending big-city opportunities with a surprisingly tight-knit, family-focused suburban feel that constantly evolves and expands.

a typical day

Sunrise to Sundown in Lehi

Morning

Active Start

Mornings in Lehi buzz with purpose. Families wake to mountain views, preparing for the day's activities. School drop-offs are efficiently managed across the city's modern campuses, while commuters hit I-15 or local routes for the short drive to tech offices in Silicon Slopes. Many grab a quick coffee at places like Beans & Brews or a local drive-thru, fueling up before diving into a productive day. The air is often crisp, and the rising sun illuminates the east-facing homes, setting a vibrant tone for the day ahead.

Afternoon

Local Hubs

Afternoons in Lehi often involve a shift from work and school to recreation and errands. Kids flock to the many parks, like Lehi Legacy Center, for sports practice or playground fun. Shoppers explore the Traverse Mountain Outlets or local retail centers for daily needs. Post-school activities are plentiful, from dance classes to tutoring centers, reflecting the city's family-centric focus. The late afternoon sun casts long shadows, signaling a gentle transition towards evening as the community unwinds from the day's hustle.

Evening

Family Time

Evenings in Lehi are typically family-focused and community-oriented. Dinner often involves cooking at home, enjoying a meal at one of the city's diverse restaurants, or grabbing takeout. Many families take evening strolls through their neighborhoods or utilize community amenities. Local events, often hosted by Thanksgiving Point or the Lehi City Arts Council, provide entertainment options. The city lights twinkle against the dark mountains, offering a peaceful backdrop as residents relax and prepare for the next day's opportunities in this bustling, growing city.

the local pulse

Schools, Dining & Weekend Escapes

The Education Standard

Lehi is proudly served by the Alpine School District, renowned for its commitment to academic excellence. The city is home to several highly-rated schools, including Lehi High School, Skyridge High School, Willowcreek Middle School, and numerous elementary schools like Vista Heights and Fox Hollow, accommodating its rapidly growing population. Infrastructure is modern and continually expanding, with easy access to I-15, which bisects the city and provides vital transportation links to Salt Lake City and Provo. Public transit options, including the UVX express bus service, connect residents to key employment and education hubs, ensuring convenient commutes and access to broader regional amenities.

Local Dining & Culture

Lehi's dining scene reflects its modern, growing character, offering a diverse array of options from casual eats to more refined experiences. Local favorites include DP Cheesesteaks, known for its authentic East Coast-style sandwiches, and The Roll Up Crepes, a popular spot for sweet and savory delights. For something refreshing, Rockwell Ice Cream is a must-visit. The Traverse Mountain area boasts numerous chain restaurants and fast-casual options like Café Rio and In-N-Out Burger, catering to every craving. From family-friendly diners to trendy new cafes, Lehi's culinary landscape is continually evolving to match its dynamic population.

Favorite Weekend Spots

Weekends in Lehi are packed with opportunities for adventure and relaxation. Thanksgiving Point is a major draw, featuring the Museum of Natural Curiosity, Farm Country, Ashton Gardens, and Dinosaur Island, offering hours of family fun and educational experiences. Outdoor enthusiasts can explore the extensive trail systems in nearby Traverse Mountain for hiking and mountain biking, or head south to Utah Lake for boating and fishing. For shopping and entertainment, the Traverse Mountain Outlets provide a wide selection of retail therapy, complemented by movie theaters and various dining establishments. Community parks, like Lehi Legacy Park, are perfect for picnics and sports, ensuring there's always something engaging to do, whether you're seeking nature, culture, or retail enjoyment.
