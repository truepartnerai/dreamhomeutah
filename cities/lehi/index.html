What Makes Lehi Different

Lehi is the vibrant heart of 'Silicon Slopes,' offering abundant tech job opportunities, highly-rated schools, and a family-friendly atmosphere. Residents enjoy modern master-planned communities, extensive outdoor recreation with stunning mountain views, and world-class amenities at Thanksgiving Point. Its strategic location provides convenient access to both Salt Lake City and Provo.

A Day in Lehi: The Local Routine

AM

Morning: Active Start

Mornings in Lehi begin with a busy energy, as commuters head to I-15 for work in tech companies or further afield. School drop-offs create bustling neighborhood streets, while local coffee shops like Beans & Brews fill up with early risers. Some take advantage of the cool air for an early run or a workout at a local gym.

12P

Afternoon: Local Hubs

Afternoons see a mix of professional and recreational activities. Tech workers often enjoy lunch at one of the many eateries around Thanksgiving Point or within their corporate campuses. Children spill out of schools and head to numerous parks, community centers, or organized after-school activities. Shoppers frequent the Traverse Mountain Outlets, and the city's ongoing growth means construction is often visible in new developments.

PM

Evening: Family & Community

Evenings in Lehi are typically family-focused. Dinners are shared at home or at popular local restaurants such as Porter's Smokehouse or Adobe Grill. Many families enjoy community events at Thanksgiving Point, high school sports games, or performing arts shows. Neighborhoods come alive with evening strolls, and the mountain backdrop provides a serene setting for winding down.

Schools, Dining & Local Infrastructure

The Education Standard

Lehi falls primarily within the highly-regarded Alpine School District, known for its excellent schools and commitment to growth. The city is a hub for major transportation arteries, including I-15 and Timpanogos Highway (SR-92), facilitating regional commutes. Public transit via UTA buses and nearby FrontRunner commuter rail further enhances connectivity. The city continuously invests in infrastructure to support its rapid expansion.

Local Dining & Culture

Lehi's dining scene is diverse and growing, reflecting its burgeoning population. It offers a blend of casual family dining, fast-casual eateries, and some more upscale options. Thanksgiving Point is a culinary destination with several unique restaurants like Harvest Restaurant. Local favorites include Porter's Smokehouse BBQ, Adobe Grill for Mexican cuisine, and numerous national chains alongside local gems serving everything from sushi to artisan sandwiches.

Favorite Weekend Spots

Weekends in Lehi are packed with options. Thanksgiving Point is a major draw, offering the Museum of Natural Curiosity, Ashton Gardens, and Farm Country. Shoppers flock to Traverse Mountain Outlets, while outdoor enthusiasts explore hiking and biking trails in the nearby mountains, or enjoy sports at the Lehi Sporting Complex. Utah Lake is a short drive for water activities, and world-class ski resorts are within an hour's reach.
