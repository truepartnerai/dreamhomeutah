12 Things You Need to Know About Lehi

A curated data profile for families and professionals looking to establish roots.

Transit & Proximity

  • 1. I-15 Corridor AccessLehi boasts prime access to Interstate 15, a major artery connecting the city seamlessly to Salt Lake City to the north and Provo to the south. This accessibility is key for commuters and regional travel.
  • 2. Silicon Slopes CentralPositioned at the heart of 'Silicon Slopes,' Lehi is home to major tech companies like Adobe, Ancestry, and Qualtrics. This creates a vibrant economic hub and numerous local job opportunities.
  • 3. Salt Lake City CommuteThe average commute time to downtown Salt Lake City is approximately 30-45 minutes, depending on traffic conditions. Many tech companies are even closer, reducing travel time significantly.
  • 4. FrontRunner ConnectivityThough not directly in Lehi, the Lehi FrontRunner station offers commuter rail service, connecting residents to Ogden, Salt Lake City, and Provo, providing an alternative to driving.
  • 5. Traverse Mountain HubThe Traverse Mountain area in Lehi is a major commercial and retail hub, featuring shopping outlets, restaurants, and entertainment. It serves as a convenient amenity for local residents.
  • 6. SLC Airport DistanceSalt Lake City International Airport (SLC) is approximately 35-45 miles north of Lehi. This offers relatively easy access for domestic and international air travel.

Demographics & Community

  • 7. Rapid Population GrowthLehi has been one of the fastest-growing cities in Utah and the nation over the last decade. Its population has soared, reflecting its economic vitality and appeal to families.
  • 8. Alpine School DistrictPublic education in Lehi falls under the highly-rated Alpine School District. The district is known for its strong academic programs and modern facilities, including Skyridge High School.
  • 9. Thanksgiving Point AnchorThanksgiving Point is a unique educational and recreational complex in Lehi, featuring museums, gardens, and farm experiences. It's a significant cultural and community landmark.
  • 10. Young, Family-Oriented CityThe demographics skew young, with a high percentage of families with children. Lehi is characterized by new developments designed to accommodate a family-centric lifestyle.
  • 11. Historic BeginningsFounded in 1850, Lehi has a rich pioneer history as one of Utah's oldest cities. While rapidly modernizing, remnants of its agricultural past can still be seen in older parts of town.
  • 12. Active Community EventsLehi hosts numerous community events throughout the year, including the annual Round-Up celebration and various festivals at Thanksgiving Point. These foster strong community bonds.

What We Love About Lehi (And You Will Too)

Lehi is a vibrant, rapidly expanding city at the heart of Utah's 'Silicon Slopes,' drawing residents with its blend of innovative tech culture and family-friendly living. The community thrives on growth, offering modern amenities, excellent schools within the Alpine School District, and abundant recreational opportunities. Its strategic location provides easy access to both Salt Lake City and Provo, making commutes manageable while offering stunning mountain and lake views. Residents appreciate the dynamic energy, diverse dining, and array of shopping options, all while maintaining a strong sense of community perfect for raising a family.

Lehi: Where Innovation Meets Modern Family Living

Lehi stands apart from many traditional Utah County cities due to its undeniable embrace of the future. While Orem or Provo retain more historical or university town charm, Lehi has rapidly transformed into a modern tech hub, defined by gleaming corporate campuses and new suburban developments. This focus on innovation fosters a more transient, diverse population drawn by career opportunities, leading to a dynamic, forward-thinking atmosphere. It might lack some of the quaint, established neighborhood feels of its southern neighbors, but it compensates with unparalleled growth, state-of-the-art infrastructure, and a constant influx of fresh energy.

Schools, Dining & Local Infrastructure

The Education Standard

Lehi is proudly served by the Alpine School District, one of Utah's largest and highest-performing districts. The city is home to several well-regarded schools, including Skyridge High School and Lehi High School, along with numerous middle and elementary schools that consistently achieve strong academic results. The city's infrastructure is modern and continues to expand, notably with excellent access to I-15, which facilitates efficient commutes north to Salt Lake City or south to Provo. Public transportation options, including FrontRunner stations nearby, further enhance connectivity, supporting the city's rapidly growing population and economic development.

Local Dining & Culture

Lehi's dining scene has blossomed alongside its tech boom, offering a diverse array of options that cater to its growing population. You'll find popular spots like Porter's Smokehouse, known for its delicious BBQ, or the flavorful Mexican cuisine at Adobe Grill. For a quick bite or a casual family meal, there are numerous fast-casual restaurants and national chains that have established a presence. The Traverse Mountain area, in particular, has become a hub for dining, with options ranging from upscale steakhouses to family-friendly eateries, ensuring there’s something to satisfy every palate, from a quick lunch to a leisurely dinner.

Favorite Weekend Spots

Weekends in Lehi are packed with opportunities for recreation and entertainment. Thanksgiving Point is a major draw, offering a variety of attractions including the Museum of Ancient Life, Ashton Gardens, and the Farm Country, perfect for family outings. Residents also frequent the Traverse Mountain Outlets for shopping deals and dining experiences. For outdoor enthusiasts, the Jordan River Parkway provides miles of scenic trails for walking, biking, and running, while easy access to the nearby Wasatch Front mountains offers incredible hiking and skiing opportunities. Utah Lake, just a short drive away, is ideal for boating and fishing, ensuring that there's always an adventure waiting right around the corner.
