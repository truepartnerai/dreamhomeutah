12 Things to Know
About Lehi

A curated profile for families and professionals looking to put down roots here.

Transit & Proximity

  • 1. I-15 Corridor AccessLehi sits directly on the I-15 freeway, offering direct access for commutes north to Salt Lake City and south to Provo. This prime location is essential for regional travel.
  • 2. Silicon Slopes HubLocated at the heart of Utah's "Silicon Slopes," Lehi provides immediate proximity to major tech employers like Adobe, Ancestry.com, and Oracle. This drives job growth and economic vitality.
  • 3. SR-92 ConnectivityState Route 92 (Timpanogos Highway) is a key east-west arterial, connecting Lehi residents efficiently to Saratoga Springs and Highland. It facilitates movement across the city and beyond.
  • 4. Mountain View CorridorThe Mountain View Corridor provides an alternative north-south route on Lehi's western side, easing traffic on I-15 for local commuters. It improves connectivity within western Utah County.
  • 5. Salt Lake CommuteThe average commute time from Lehi to downtown Salt Lake City is typically around 30-40 minutes, depending on traffic conditions. This makes it a feasible option for those working in the capital.
  • 6. Provo ProximityLehi is approximately 15-20 minutes north of downtown Provo, home to Brigham Young University. This offers convenient access to educational and cultural opportunities further south.

Demographics & Community

  • 7. Rapid Population GrowthLehi is one of the fastest-growing cities in Utah, with its population surging from around 19,000 in 2000 to over 85,000 today. This growth fuels continuous development and amenities.
  • 8. Alpine School DistrictLehi is served by the Alpine School District, one of the largest and highest-performing districts in Utah. It boasts numerous highly-rated schools from elementary to high school.
  • 9. Youthful DemographicsThe city has a relatively young population, with a significant number of families and children. This contributes to a vibrant, family-friendly community atmosphere and demand for schools and parks.
  • 10. Tech Industry FocusLehi is synonymous with Utah's tech industry, hosting a large concentration of technology companies. This creates a highly educated workforce and diverse economic opportunities for residents.
  • 11. Historic Lehi Round-UpThe annual Lehi Round-Up, celebrated since 1937, is a beloved tradition featuring a rodeo, parade, and carnival. It reflects the city's rich pioneer heritage and strong community spirit.
  • 12. Thanksgiving Point LandmarkThis expansive non-profit institution is a major cultural and recreational asset in Lehi, offering museums, gardens, and farm experiences. It attracts visitors from across the state and beyond.

a typical day

Sunrise to Sundown in Lehi

Morning

Active Start

A Lehi morning often kicks off with the bustling energy of commutes to Silicon Slopes tech campuses, a quick drive with stunning mountain views. Parents navigate school drop-offs at modern Alpine School District campuses, while others might grab a coffee at a local spot like Perks & Provisions before diving into their workday. The air is crisp, and the sun rising over the Wasatch Front provides a beautiful backdrop to the start of a productive day in this rapidly growing city.

Afternoon

Local Hubs

Afternoons in Lehi often revolve around family activities and leveraging the city's amenities. Kids spill out of school, heading to one of Lehi's many parks, like Lehi Sports Park or Dry Creek Park, for sports or playtime. Shopping runs at Traverse Mountain Outlets or local boutiques are common, and community centers offer a range of after-school programs. The late afternoon sun illuminates the valley, inviting residents outdoors for walks, bike rides, or enjoying the lively atmosphere.

Evening

Family Time

Evenings in Lehi typically involve winding down with family and friends. Many families gather for dinner at home, or venture out to one of the diverse dining options near Traverse Mountain. Community events, especially during warmer months, might include concerts in the park or local festivals. The city lights twinkle against the dark mountains, offering a peaceful close to a busy day, with a strong sense of community radiating from its vibrant neighborhoods.

the local pulse

Schools, Dining & Weekend Escapes

The Education Standard

Lehi is proudly part of the highly-regarded Alpine School District, one of Utah's largest and most comprehensive districts. The city boasts several excellent schools, including Skyridge High School and Lehi High School, both known for strong academic and athletic programs. Elementary and junior high options are numerous and modern, catering to the city's rapidly expanding population. Infrastructure here is constantly evolving to support growth, with significant investments in road networks like I-15 and SR-92, and developing public transit options to connect residents efficiently within Silicon Slopes and beyond. The focus is on ensuring smooth commutes and access to vital services.

Local Dining & Culture

Lehi's dining scene offers a delightful blend of casual favorites and emerging culinary gems. For a unique experience, The Block Restaurant near Thanksgiving Point provides an upscale American menu with a focus on fresh ingredients and a lively atmosphere. Craving healthy and fresh? Aubergine & Company is a local favorite for its acai bowls, smoothies, and wholesome Mediterranean-inspired dishes. The Traverse Mountain area also hosts popular chains and fast-casual options like Costa Vida and R&R BBQ. From quick bites to sit-down meals, Lehi’s dining culture is growing to match its dynamic population, offering something for every palate.

Favorite Weekend Spots

Weekends in Lehi are packed with opportunities for fun and relaxation. Thanksgiving Point is a major draw, offering everything from the Museum of Natural Curiosity to the Ashton Gardens and Butterfly Biosphere, perfect for families and nature lovers. For shopping and entertainment, the Traverse Mountain Outlets provide a wide array of stores and dining options. Outdoor enthusiasts can explore nearby hiking and biking trails in the mountains or enjoy activities around Utah Lake. The Lehi Sports Park is always bustling with youth sports, and local community events, especially during seasonal holidays like the Lehi Round-Up, provide a strong sense of community and celebration.
