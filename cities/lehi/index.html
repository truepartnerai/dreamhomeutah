12 Things You
Need to Know
About Lehi.

A curated data profile for families and professionals looking to establish roots here.

Transit & Proximity

  • 1. I-15 Access HubLehi sits strategically along I-15, providing direct access to both Salt Lake City (approx. 30 miles north) and Provo (approx. 15 miles south), making commutes efficient.
  • 2. FrontRunner StationThe city is home to a UTA FrontRunner commuter rail station, offering a convenient alternative for travel between Ogden and Provo.
  • 3. Redwood Road ArteryRedwood Road (SR-68) is a significant north-south arterial, connecting Lehi residents to Saratoga Springs, Eagle Mountain, and other western communities.
  • 4. Timpanogos HighwaySR-92, known as Timpanogos Highway, links Lehi directly to Highland and Alpine, also serving as a gateway to American Fork Canyon.
  • 5. Silicon Slopes CoreLehi is considered the heart of "Silicon Slopes," housing major tech companies like Adobe, Microsoft, and Ancestry.com, significantly reducing commute times for local employees.
  • 6. Quick Airport AccessSalt Lake City International Airport (SLC) is approximately a 35-40 minute drive, making national and international travel highly accessible.

Demographics & Community

  • 7. Rapid Population GrowthLehi has experienced explosive growth, with its population increasing from around 19,000 in 2000 to over 85,000 residents today, making it one of Utah's fastest-growing cities.
  • 8. Alpine School DistrictEducational services are provided by the Alpine School District, one of the largest districts in Utah, known for its comprehensive curriculum and modern facilities.
  • 9. Thanksgiving Point LandmarkThanksgiving Point is a prominent 500-acre non-profit farm, garden, and museum complex that offers year-round educational and recreational opportunities.
  • 10. Pioneer HeritageFounded in 1850, Lehi is one of Utah's oldest settlements, originally named Dry Creek by its pioneer settlers before being renamed for Book of Mormon prophet Lehi.
  • 11. Young DemographicThe city boasts a notably young population, with a median age often in the mid-20s, reflecting its appeal to young families and professionals.
  • 12. High HomeownershipLehi has a high rate of homeownership, indicating a stable and invested community with a strong emphasis on family-oriented living.

The Take

Lehi: Utah's Tech Frontier with Heart

Lehi distinguishes itself from other Utah County cities through its unparalleled embrace of the future, driven by the Silicon Slopes phenomenon. While Provo has its established university town charm and Spanish Fork offers a more rural feel, Lehi is a vibrant hub of innovation, rapid development, and youthful energy. It attracts a demographic eager for career advancement and modern amenities, shaping a unique culture focused on progress and convenience. This isn't your grandparent's Utah County; it's a dynamic, evolving landscape where new opportunities emerge daily, making it a pivotal player in the state's economic landscape.

A Day in the Life

Sunrise to Sundown in Lehi.

AM

Morning: Active Start

A typical Lehi morning often begins with the glow of the sun hitting the Wasatch Front peaks. Families prepare for school, with students heading to one of Alpine SD's modern campuses. Commuters, many bound for nearby tech campuses in Silicon Slopes, navigate I-15 or local routes. Coffee shops like Black Rifle Coffee Company or Beans & Brew provide a quick caffeine fix, while others enjoy a quiet breakfast before tackling their day, often discussing the latest community news or weekend plans.

12P

Afternoon: Local Hubs

Afternoons in Lehi buzz with activity. Kids often head to one of the many community parks, like Ivory Ridge Park or VASA Park, for sports practices or playtime. Shoppers explore the Outlets at Traverse Mountain for retail therapy or grab essentials at local grocery stores. Many professionals enjoy lunch breaks at various eateries around their tech campuses, while others might fit in a quick workout at a local gym or enjoy a scenic walk on a neighborhood trail, soaking in the open space.

PM

Evening: Family & Community

As evening descends, Lehi homes fill with the warmth of family life. Dinner often involves cooking at home or enjoying a meal at a local restaurant, perhaps Slab Pizza for a casual night out. Many residents attend school events, community sports leagues, or church activities. The vibrant lights of Thanksgiving Point often draw families for a stroll or an evening show. Cooler temperatures bring a peaceful close to the day, with mountain views providing a serene backdrop.

The Local Pulse

Schools, Dining & Weekend Escapes.

The Education Standard

Lehi falls under the highly-regarded Alpine School District, one of Utah's largest and most progressive. The city boasts state-of-the-art educational facilities, including prominent high schools like Skyridge High School and Lehi High School, along with numerous middle and elementary schools designed to support a rapidly growing population. Key arterial roads such as I-15, Redwood Road, and SR-92 (Timpanogos Highway) provide excellent connectivity, ensuring smooth commutes for residents throughout the valley and into neighboring cities. Public transit options, including UTA FrontRunner and bus services, further enhance accessibility, making Lehi a well-connected and infrastructurally robust community.

Local Dining & Culture

Lehi's dining culture is as dynamic as its growth, offering a diverse array of options from quick bites to sit-down experiences. For classic American fare and legendary shakes, JCW's The Burger Family is a local institution. Pizza lovers flock to Slab Pizza for their unique, rectangular slices and fresh ingredients. Craving something different? HallPass at The Point offers a vibrant food hall experience with multiple vendors. You'll also find popular chains, authentic Mexican cuisine, and Asian eateries spread throughout the city, reflecting the diverse palates of its burgeoning population. There’s always something new and delicious to discover, from casual family dinners to more upscale date nights.

Favorite Weekend Spots

Weekends in Lehi are an adventure, with endless opportunities for recreation and relaxation. Thanksgiving Point is a major draw, featuring world-class attractions like the Museum of Natural Curiosity, Farm Country, the Butterfly Biosphere, and stunning themed gardens perfect for family outings. For shopping enthusiasts, the Outlets at Traverse Mountain offer a wide selection of retail brands. Outdoor lovers can explore nearby mountain trails for hiking and biking, or head to Utah Lake State Park for boating, fishing, and paddleboarding. Community parks frequently host local sports leagues and events, fostering a vibrant social scene. Whether it’s educational fun, retail therapy, or connecting with nature, Lehi offers a rich tapestry of weekend activities for all ages.
