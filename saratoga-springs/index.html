12 Things You Need to Know About Lehi

A curated data profile for families and professionals looking to establish roots.

Transit & Proximity

  • 1. Direct I-15 Arteries Built around 2 major high-capacity transit access points (Main St and Timpanogos Hwy/SR-92) ensuring seamless highway connection.
  • 2. The BYU Corridor Exactly 20 minutes south to Brigham Young University and downtown Provo.
  • 3. Salt Lake City Access Exactly 30 minutes north to downtown Salt Lake City, perfect for major cultural events or corporate commutes.
  • 4. Work-From-Home Stronghold An estimated 24% of the local workforce works entirely from home, keeping cars off the road outside peak times.
  • 5. Historic Commute Pace Average individual drive time to local employment zones clocks in right around 23.7 minutes.
  • 6. Mountain Biking & Trail Access Directly borders the Murdock Canal Trail, a 31-mile continuous paved multi-use track looping the valley.

Demographics & Economy

  • 7. The 100K Milestone Lehi has officially crossed a major population threshold, sitting at an estimated 101,737 residents.
  • 8. High Purchasing Power The median household income is an impressive $131,299, reflecting the high-paying corporate landscape nearby.
  • 9. Deep Historical Roots Incorporated on February 5, 1852, Lehi holds the historical title of being Utah's 6th oldest city.
  • 10. Hyper-Youthful Vibe With a median age of just 26.9 years, it is ranked as one of the youngest and most vibrant suburban populations in the state.
  • 11. Higher Education Standard A staggering 50.0% of adult residents hold a Bachelor's degree or higher, far exceeding the national average.
  • 12. Hollywood’s Footloose Legacy The iconic 1905 Lehi Roller Mills served as the direct backdrop for the 1984 Paramount film *Footloose*, starring Kevin Bacon.

What We Love About Lehi (And You Will Too)

What absolutely hooks us about Lehi is its incredible structural adaptability. It’s a place where you can run a high-level tech consultancy or drop into a corporate office on the Slopes, and within fifteen minutes, find yourself completely unplugged on high-end mountain biking singletrack up at Lambert Park or navigating the Corner Canyon trail networks. The master planners here didn't just build subdivisions; they intelligently threaded micro-parks, continuous paved paths, and world-class destination amenities like Thanksgiving Point right into the residential grid. It gives the entire area a distinct, high-energy momentum where you never have to sacrifice outdoor lifestyle quality for metropolitan economic career growth.

Schools, Dining & Local Infrastructure

The Education Standard

Lehi falls inside the highly regarded Alpine School District. High-performing public icons like Skyridge High School and Lehi High School anchor the secondary tracks with outstanding academic and athletic indices.

Local Dining & Culture

From long-standing institutions like the historic Lehi Roller Mills (proudly supplying local flour since 1905) to modern casual dining spots like Chubby's Cafe, the local culinary ecosystem handles both heritage staples and corporate lunch crowds easily.

Weekend Destination Hub: Thanksgiving Point

The cultural cornerstone of the area is Thanksgiving Point. This sprawling complex serves as a massive recreational destination housing the Museum of Natural Curiosity, Ashton Gardens, and a world-renowned dinosaur museum—perfectly curating weekend experiences for active families.
